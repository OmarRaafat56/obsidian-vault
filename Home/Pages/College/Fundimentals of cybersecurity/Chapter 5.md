#  Physical and Infrastructure Security

## 📌 **1. Types of Information System (IS) Security**
- **Logical Security**: Protects data from software and network-based threats.
- **Physical (Infrastructure) Security**: Protects hardware, facilities, and people.
- **Premises (Facilities) Security**: Protects buildings, people, property. Includes perimeter security, fire protection, surveillance, guards.

---

## 🎯 **2. Role of Physical Security**
- **Goals**:
    1. Prevent damage to physical infrastructure.
    2. Prevent misuse leading to data compromise.
- **Protected Assets**:
    - Hardware (servers, storage, network devices)
    - Facilities (buildings, rooms)
    - Supporting services (power, HVAC, communications)
    - Personnel

---

## 📍 **3. System Operating Locations**
- **Static systems**: Fixed installations (main focus).
- **Mobile systems**: Installed in vehicles.
- **Portable systems**: Laptops, mobile devices.
- **Location affects threat type and severity**.

---

## 🚨 **4. Categories of Physical Security Threats**
1. **Environmental Threats**
2. **Technical Threats**
3. **Human-Caused Threats**

---

## 🌍 **5. Environmental Threats**
- **Temperature & Humidity**: Safe range ≈ 10–32°C; high humidity damages electronics.
- **Fire & Smoke**: Flame, heat, toxic fumes, water from sprinklers.
- **Water Damage**: Plumbing leaks, floods.
- **Chemical / Biological / Radiological Hazards**: Accidental or intentional.
- **Dust**: Damages hardware.
- **Infestation**: Mold, insects, rodents.

### 🛡️ **Prevention**:
- Climate control & sensors
- Fire alarms/suppression
- Water sensors & automatic power cutoff
- Proper equipment placement
- Filters & cleaning
- Pest control
- Training & emergency procedures

---

## ⚡ **6. Technical Threats**
- **Electrical Power Problems**:
    - Under-voltage (brownouts, outages)
    - Over-voltage (surges, lightning)
    - Noise (causes logic errors)
- **Electromagnetic Interference (EMI)**: From motors, radios, nearby equipment.

### 🛡️ **Protection**:
- UPS, generators
- Shielding, power-line filtering
- Infrastructure-aware design

---

## 👤 **7. Human-Caused Threats**
- **Unauthorized Physical Access**: Theft, vandalism, misuse.
- **Theft**: Equipment, data (copying, eavesdropping, wiretapping).
- **Vandalism**: Destruction of equipment/data.
- **Misuse**: Improper use by authorized/unauthorized users.

---

## 🔒 **11. Physical Access Control**
- **Protects**: IT hardware, wiring, power systems, HVAC, backup media, documents.
- **Methods**:
    1. Restricted buildings & guarded areas
    2. Locked rooms, cabinets, safes
    3. Securing devices to fixed objects
    4. Power-switch security devices
    5. Tracking devices for movable assets
    6. Continuous tracking for portable assets
- Use **sensors & alarms** for detection.

---

## 🛠️ **12. Recovery from Physical Security Breaches**
- **Redundancy is essential**.
- Off-site backups (near real-time)
- Encrypted network backups
- Hot sites for failover
- Disaster recovery specialists
- Remove residual hazards (fire, water, smoke)

---

## 📋 **13. Physical Security Planning Process**
1. Form a steering committee
2. Gather historical data & expert advice
3. Identify all possible threats
4. Estimate likelihood of each threat
5. Estimate direct costs
6. Consider cascading impacts
7. Prioritize threats
8. Produce a threat assessment report

---

## 🔗 **14. Integration of Physical & Logical Security**
- Centralized monitoring of alarms/sensors
- Unified access control systems
- Smart cards & automated authentication
- Increases effectiveness & reduces cost

---

## 🪪 **15. FIPS 201-1 (PIV Standard)**
- U.S. standard for identity verification.
- Uses **PIV smart cards**.
- Supports: Physical + Logical access.
- **Authentication factors**: Card + PIN + Biometrics (fingerprint).
- **Assurance levels**:
    - Some confidence
    - High confidence
    - Very high confidence

---

## ✅ **16. Key Takeaways**
- Physical security is the **foundation of IS security**.
- Threats are **environmental, technical, and human**.
- **Prevention, detection, redundancy, and recovery** are critical.
- **Integration with logical security** is increasingly important.
